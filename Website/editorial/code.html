The 2023 edition of Jukola presented one of the most physically demanding terrains in recent history. While the night legs were characterized by diffuse visibility, the long day leg offered a classic route choice problem that split the field.

In this analysis, we break down the execution of the top 5 teams on leg 4, focusing specifically on the 1.8km leg between control 12 and 13.

The Northern Route Choice

Most elite runners opted for the northern variant, aiming to minimize climb. While longer by 120 meters, it offered significantly better runnability on the hardened forest road.

However, the entry into the control circle proved tricky from the north. The vegetation density was higher than mapped, causing an average time loss of 15-20 seconds for those who didn't slow down to read the micro-features.

Comparing this to the direct line, which involved crossing two distinct marshes, the energy conservation on the northern route likely paid dividends for the subsequent technical section.

Conclusion

Ultimately, execution trumped route choice on this leg. The fastest time was actually set on the straight route by a runner who managed to hit the micro-route perfectly through the marshland.
